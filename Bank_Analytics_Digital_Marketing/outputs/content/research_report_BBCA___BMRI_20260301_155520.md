# Market Research Report: BBCA & BMRI Churn Prevention Campaign

**Date:** October 26, 2023
**Prepared For:** BBCA & BMRI Marketing & Retention Teams
**Prepared By:** Marketing Research Analyst

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This report provides comprehensive market research to support BBCA and BMRI's proactive customer churn prevention digital marketing campaign. It synthesizes insights from competitive analysis, detailed audience personas, and current industry trends within the Indonesian retail banking sector. The findings aim to inform campaign strategy, focusing on at-risk segments: "New & Nimble Churners" (short tenure, month-to-month contracts) and "High-Value Digital Users" (high monthly charges). Key recommendations include leveraging hyper-personalized digital engagement, seamless embedded financial services, proactive value enhancement, simplified contract/payment migrations, and enhanced digital customer support.

---

## 2. Competitive Landscape Analysis

The Indonesian retail banking sector is highly competitive, with major players exhibiting distinct strengths and strategic focuses. Understanding these competitors provides context for BBCA & BMRI's retention efforts.

### **Bank Mandiri**
*   **Market Positioning:** Primarily positioned as Indonesia’s leading Corporate, Investment, and Wholesale Bank, with a strong emphasis on digital transformation and sustainability ("Growth through Digital and Eco").
*   **Target Segments:** Corporate, investment, and wholesale clients. Retail strategy is less explicitly detailed in their public messaging, suggesting a potential focus on high-net-worth individuals or business owners who also require retail services.
*   **Key Messages:** Leadership in institutional banking, digital-first approach, sustainability, and future-oriented innovation.
*   **Channel Mix:** Strong digital channels for transaction banking; extensive institutional coverage and relationship management. Retail-specific channels are less highlighted.
*   **Strengths:** Strong brand reputation, digital leadership in transaction banking, clear strategic direction, robust risk management, extensive institutional reach.
*   **Weaknesses (Retail Context):** Limited explicit retail focus in messaging, ambiguity in "Eco" strategy for retail, potential institutional bias in product development.
*   **Differentiation Opportunities (for BBCA & BMRI):** Focus on hyper-personalized retail experiences, strong community engagement, niche digital retail solutions, transparent and tangible ESG for retail, superior customer service, and agile product innovation.

### **Bank Rakyat Indonesia (BRI)**
*   **Market Positioning:** "The People's Bank," leading financial partner for Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs), synonymous with financial inclusion, particularly for rural and underserved populations.
*   **Target Segments:** Primarily MSMEs, rural and semi-urban populations, and the Ultra-Micro (UMi) segment.
*   **Key Messages:** "Your Partner in Growth," "Banking for Everyone, Everywhere," "Empowering Local Economies," "Sustainable and Reliable."
*   **Channel Mix:** Unrivaled extensive agent network (BRILink, targeting >700,000 agents by 2026), co-located UMi outlets (>1,200 by 2025), traditional branch network. Digital channels are present but less emphasized in their penetration strategy.
*   **Strengths:** Unrivaled rural and MSME penetration, strong brand equity and trust, deep local market understanding, dedicated UMi focus, clear strategic direction.
*   **Weaknesses:** Potential digital lag compared to more digitally-native competitors, operational scalability challenges with vast agent network, potential under-penetration in urban/affluent markets.
*   **Differentiation Opportunities (for BBCA & BMRI):** Offer superior digital-first solutions for tech-savvy MSMEs, focus on niche urban/affluent markets, develop specialized industry solutions, and provide superior digital and hybrid customer experiences.

### **Bank Negara Indonesia (BNI)**
*   **Market Positioning:** Financially resilient and fundamentally strong retail bank, with a strategic growth area in Indonesian Sharia Banking. Emphasizes accelerated digital transformation.
*   **Target Segments:** Customers of Indonesian Sharia Banks (a clear growth focus), and a broader spectrum of Indonesian consumers and SMEs.
*   **Key Messages:** Stability and resilience, modernity and convenience (digital transformation), growth and opportunity in Sharia banking, trust and compliance.
*   **Channel Mix:** Strong emphasis on digital channels (mobile banking apps, internet banking); traditional branches and ATMs for broader reach.
*   **Strengths:** Financial resilience, disciplined funding, proactive digital transformation, clear growth strategy in Sharia banking, regulatory compliance.
*   **Weaknesses:** Exposure to macroeconomic risks (food inflation, exchange rate), not yet a dominant player in Sharia banking (implies need for aggressive competition).
*   **Differentiation Opportunities (for BBCA & BMRI):** Become a leading digital Sharia banking provider, position as a "safe haven" amidst volatility, offer tailored risk mitigation products, and provide integrated Sharia and conventional offerings.

### **CIMB Niaga**
*   **Market Positioning:** Customer-centric, innovative, and purpose-driven regional bank. Aims for Top 3 Net Promoter Score (NPS) by 2030, top quartile Return on Equity (ROE), and 45% CASA ratio. Leverages CIMB Group's "One Bank" ASEAN footprint.
*   **Target Segments:** Mass affluent and affluent customers, digitally savvy customers/young professionals, SMEs with regional aspirations, socially conscious consumers, and general retail customers.
*   **Key Messages:** "Your Partner in Progress: Advancing You and Society," "Experience Top-Tier Service," "Innovative Banking, Seamlessly Connected Across ASEAN," "Smart Solutions for Your Financial Journey," "Banking with Purpose."
*   **Channel Mix:** Hybrid strategy with strong emphasis on digital platforms (mobile apps, online platforms); optimized physical branches for complex transactions/relationship management; dedicated relationship managers; integrated call centers.
*   **Strengths:** Clear strategic vision and ambitious goals, strong customer-centricity (NPS focus), financial discipline, innovation and integration focus ("One Bank" model), regional backing, purpose-driven brand.
*   **Weaknesses:** High execution risk for ambitious targets, intense competition, potential legacy system integration challenges, potential brand perception gap, dependence on group strategy.
*   **Differentiation Opportunities (for BBCA & BMRI):** Achieve and market top-tier customer experience, offer seamless cross-border banking for specific niches, lead with innovation in niche products, demonstrate tangible social impact, provide holistic wealth management with regional insights, and implement data-driven personalization at scale.

## 3. Target Audience Personas

To effectively address churn, we have developed three personas representing the identified at-risk segments.

### Persona 1: "The Digital Nomad" (Segment 2: New & Nimble Churners)

*   **Overview:** Young, tech-savvy professional valuing flexibility, convenience, and seamless digital experiences. Low loyalty, quick to switch for better digital offerings, lower fees, or attractive rewards.
*   **Demographics:** 25-30 years old, IDR 8-15 million/month income, urban centers, Bachelor's degree, digital/tech/creative occupations.
*   **Psychographics:** Values convenience, efficiency, innovation, transparency, freedom. Fast-paced, digitally connected lifestyle. Skeptical of traditional institutions, expects seamless digital, values peer recommendations, price-sensitive.
*   **Pain Points:** Slow/outdated mobile apps, hidden fees, lack of instant customer support, need for physical branch visits, feeling undervalued, limited integration with other digital services, irrelevant marketing.
*   **Goals:** Effortless on-the-go finance management, maximize savings/investments with minimal effort, quick/secure payments, bank that understands digital lifestyle, relevant rewards, financial independence.
*   **Preferred Channels:** In-app notifications, WhatsApp Business, email (updates), social media (Instagram, Twitter), online forums.
*   **Decision Factors:** Mobile app UX/UI, low/transparent fees, sign-up bonuses/cashback, digital integration, transaction speed, peer recommendations, innovative features.
*   **Content Preferences:** Short videos, infographics, blog posts (fintech, personal finance), interactive tools, relatable social media, user-generated content.

### Persona 2: "The Savvy Family Planner" (Segment 2: New & Nimble Churners, potentially transitioning to Segment 1)

*   **Overview:** Responsible individual, often with a young family, focused on financial security. Pragmatic, value-conscious, seeking reliability and clear benefits. Uses digital banking but appreciates good customer service and clear communication. Sensitive to fees, will switch for better value or unmet family needs.
*   **Demographics:** 32-40 years old, IDR 12-25 million/month income, suburban areas/secondary cities, Bachelor's/Master's degree, mid-level manager, civil servant, small business owner.
*   **Psychographics:** Values security, stability, family well-being, value for money, reliability, trust, long-term planning. Busy with family, focuses on budgeting, saving for milestones. Pragmatic, moderately risk-averse, values good customer service, highly sensitive to hidden costs.
*   **Pain Points:** High fees, unclear product information, difficulty managing family accounts, inconsistent customer service, feeling pressured, security concerns, lack of personalized advice.
*   **Goals:** Secure family's financial future, save for major life events, good interest rates/loan products, simplify household finance, reliable financial advice, safe and growing money.
*   **Preferred Channels:** Email (detailed info), WhatsApp Business (quick queries), phone calls (complex issues), bank website, occasional branch visits.
*   **Decision Factors:** Competitive rates, low/waived fees, bank reputation/trustworthiness, comprehensive family-focused products, customer service quality, ease of family finance management, robust security.
*   **Content Preferences:** Detailed articles/guides (financial planning), webinars, comparison charts, testimonials, email newsletters (tips, offers), security-focused content.

### Persona 3: "The Ambitious Entrepreneur" (Segment 1: High-Value Digital Users)

*   **Overview:** High-achieving individual (SME owner, senior executive) with significant income/investments. Demanding, expects premium service, advanced digital tools, and personalized solutions. High-value but conditional loyalty based on exceptional value, efficiency, and proactive support.
*   **Demographics:** 30-45 years old, IDR 25 million+/month income, major business districts, Bachelor's/Master's (business/finance/engineering), startup founder, SME owner, investor.
*   **Psychographics:** Values efficiency, innovation, growth, exclusivity, recognition, time-saving, strategic partnerships, control. Demanding, results-oriented, expects premium/proactive service, values personalized attention, analytical, seeks competitive advantage.
*   **Pain Points:** Generic solutions, lack of dedicated relationship manager, slow processing for high-value transactions, inadequate business digital tools, high monthly charges without perceived value, difficulty integrating personal/business finances, lack of proactive advice.
*   **Goals:** Optimize business operations, grow personal wealth, access exclusive products/insights, receive personalized/proactive advice, save time with efficient digital tools, build trusted bank relationship, networking opportunities.
*   **Preferred Channels:** Dedicated relationship manager (phone, email, secure messaging, in-person), secure in-app messaging, email (reports, market updates), exclusive online portals, WhatsApp Business (quick queries with RM).
*   **Decision Factors:** Dedicated RM, exclusive benefits/perks, advanced digital banking (especially business), competitive rates (deposits, loans, investments), wealth management reputation, seamless personal/business integration, proactive advice/insights.
*   **Content Preferences:** Exclusive market reports, investment analyses, invitations to private webinars/events, case studies, personalized financial planning, content on advanced digital features, thought leadership.

## 4. Industry Trends and Strategic Opportunities

The Indonesian retail banking sector is experiencing significant growth and digital transformation, presenting both challenges and opportunities for churn prevention.

### Current Market Size and Growth Rate
*   **Overall Market Growth:** Projected to grow from **USD 1.32 trillion in 2025 to USD 1.95 trillion by 2031 (CAGR of 6.8%)**, indicating a robust and expanding sector.
*   **Core Banking Solution Investment:** Approximately **USD 1.4 billion**, driven by digital banking adoption.
*   **Embedded Finance Expansion:** Expected to reach **US$8.82 billion by 2025 (11.3% annual growth)**.

### Top 5 Emerging Trends for Retention Marketing in Banking

1.  **Hyper-Personalized Digital Engagement:** Leveraging advanced data analytics and AI to deliver highly customized offers, communications, and product recommendations through digital channels (in-app, email, WhatsApp). This directly addresses the "Digital Nomad" and "Ambitious Entrepreneur" personas' demand for tailored experiences and the "Savvy Family Planner's" need for relevant information.
2.  **Seamless Embedded Financial Services:** Integrating retention-focused financial solutions directly into customers' daily digital ecosystems (e-commerce, social media, lifestyle apps). This could involve offering instant, personalized credit options at the point of need, or loyalty rewards redeemable across various platforms, making banking an invisible, convenient part of their lives. This aligns with the "Explosive Growth in Embedded Finance" and the "Digital Nomad's" preference for integrated services.
3.  **Proactive Value Enhancement & Loyalty Programs:** Moving beyond basic transactional relationships to continuously demonstrate value, especially for high-value customers and those with higher monthly charges. This includes tiered loyalty programs, exclusive access to premium features, and proactive financial advice. This addresses the "Ambitious Entrepreneur's" demand for exclusivity and the "Savvy Family Planner's" need for value.
4.  **Simplified & Rewarding Contract/Payment Migrations:** Designing campaigns that make it easy and attractive for customers to commit to longer-term contracts and adopt efficient payment methods. This involves clear incentives (discounts, cashback) and frictionless digital processes, directly targeting churn drivers like "month-to-month contracts" and "manual payment methods." This appeals to "The Savvy Family Planner's" value consciousness and "The Digital Nomad's" desire for efficiency.
5.  **Enhanced Digital Customer Support & Financial Literacy:** Providing instant, intelligent, and personalized support through digital channels (AI chatbots, in-app chat, WhatsApp) to resolve issues quickly and offer proactive financial guidance. This also includes educational content (webinars, articles, infographics) that empowers customers to make informed decisions, building trust and reducing churn, especially for newer customers or those with complex needs. This addresses pain points across all personas, particularly "The Digital Nomad's" expectation of instant support and "The Savvy Family Planner's" need for clear information.

### Technology Disruptions
*   **Digital-First Banking Platforms:** Heavy investment in robust online and mobile banking.
*   **Core Banking System Modernization:** Shift from legacy systems to agile, API-driven architectures.
*   **Embedded Finance Integration:** Financial services moving beyond traditional interfaces into daily consumer platforms.
*   **Data Analytics and AI:** Disrupting credit scoring, personalization, and fraud detection.

### Consumer Behaviour Shifts
*   **Preference for Digital Channels:** Expectation to conduct banking activities digitally.
*   **Demand for Instant Gratification and Seamless Experiences:** Desire for integrated, effortless financial services.
*   **Increased Appetite for Consumption Credit:** Driven by easier digital access and a growing middle class.
*   **Expectation of Personalization:** Demand for tailored products and services based on data.

### Regulatory Changes
*   **OJK Oversight and Data Transparency:** Crucial role in monitoring and regulating, ensuring market stability.
*   **Evolving Frameworks for Digital and Embedded Finance:** New regulations for data privacy, consumer protection, and cybersecurity are anticipated.

### Untapped Opportunities
*   **Hyper-Personalized Digital Products:** Leveraging analytics for customized savings, investment, or lending.
*   **Expansion of Embedded Finance Ecosystems:** Beyond e-commerce, into healthcare, education, real estate.
*   **Financial Inclusion through Digital Channels:** Reaching underserved populations with basic banking and micro-solutions.
*   **SME Digital Lending and Support:** Digital-first lending solutions integrated with SME operational platforms.
*   **Wealth Management for the Emerging Affluent:** Accessible, digital-first wealth management products.

### Key Success Factors for Campaigns
*   **Digital-First Engagement:** Campaigns designed for mobile apps, social media, online platforms.
*   **Highlighting Convenience and Seamless Integration:** Emphasizing effortless integration into daily activities.
*   **Addressing Lifestyle and Aspiration:** Resonating with consumer desires and enabling desired lifestyles.
*   **Building Trust and Security:** Proactively addressing data privacy and transaction security concerns.
*   **Strategic Partnerships:** Collaborating with popular non-financial platforms and influencers.
*   **Clear Value Proposition:** Demonstrating tangible ROI and enhanced customer experience.

## 5. Strategic Opportunities for BBCA & BMRI Churn Prevention

Based on the comprehensive market research, BBCA & BMRI have significant opportunities to proactively prevent churn by aligning their campaign strategies with identified trends and audience needs.

1.  **Deepen Digital Personalization for "New & Nimble Churners":**
    *   **Opportunity:** Leverage the "Accelerated Digital Banking Adoption" trend to deliver highly personalized onboarding journeys and early engagement offers (like "Welcome Bonus") via in-app notifications and personalized emails.
    *   **Action:** Implement AI-driven analytics to identify early signs of disengagement in Segment 2 and trigger automated, tailored interventions (e.g., a personalized message highlighting unused features or a small, exclusive cashback offer for continued usage).
    *   **Alignment:** Addresses "The Digital Nomad's" demand for seamless digital experiences and "The Savvy Family Planner's" need for clear value.

2.  **Incentivize Long-Term Commitment through Value-Added Bundles:**
    *   **Opportunity:** Capitalize on the "Simplified & Rewarding Contract/Payment Migrations" trend to convert month-to-month contracts.
    *   **Action:** Enhance "Upgrade & Hemat" with exclusive bundles that combine banking services with lifestyle benefits (e.g., discounts on streaming services, e-commerce vouchers, or premium digital security features) for customers who commit to 12-month or 24-month contracts.
    *   **Alignment:** Directly targets the "Contract Type" churn driver and appeals to "The Savvy Family Planner's" value consciousness and "The Ambitious Entrepreneur's" desire for exclusive benefits.

3.  **Integrate "AutoPay" with Lifestyle Rewards:**
    *   **Opportunity:** Leverage the "Explosive Growth in Embedded Finance" and "Sustained Consumption Loan Growth" trends to boost "Auto-Payment Adoption."
    *   **Action:** Expand "AutoPay Anti-Ribet" to offer cashback or loyalty points that are redeemable for popular lifestyle purchases (e.g., food delivery, ride-hailing, online entertainment) when customers activate automatic payments. Partner with key Indonesian digital platforms.
    *   **Alignment:** Addresses the "Payment Method" churn driver and appeals to "The Digital Nomad's" desire for integrated services and "The Savvy Family Planner's" need for practical rewards.

4.  **Elevate "High-Value Digital Users" with Proactive Wealth & Digital Security:**
    *   **Opportunity:** Address the "Monthly Charges" churn driver for Segment 1 by enhancing perceived value through "Proactive Value Enhancement & Loyalty Programs."
    *   **Action:** For "The Ambitious Entrepreneur" and high-value "Savvy Family Planners," offer a "Digital Pro Upgrade" that includes not just premium security features but also proactive, personalized financial advisory services (e.g., investment insights, tax planning tips) delivered through a dedicated digital channel or relationship manager.
    *   **Alignment:** Directly addresses the "Monthly Charges" churn driver and meets "The Ambitious Entrepreneur's" demand for premium service and strategic guidance.

5.  **Build Early Loyalty with "Welcome Bonus" and Digital Financial Literacy:**
    *   **Opportunity:** Address the "Tenure" churn driver by focusing on early engagement and "Enhanced Digital Customer Support & Financial Literacy."
    *   **Action:** For new customers (tenure < 3 months), combine the "Welcome Bonus" with access to exclusive digital financial literacy modules (e.g., short video series on budgeting, investing basics, understanding bank fees) and a dedicated in-app chat support for new users.
    *   **Alignment:** Targets "Segment 2: New & Nimble Churners" by building trust and providing value beyond just transactions, appealing to "The Digital Nomad's" need for clear information and "The Savvy Family Planner's" desire for financial security.
